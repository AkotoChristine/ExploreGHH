Festivals and Events

Immerse yourself in the colorful traditions, music, and stories of Ghana. From historic festivals honoring our ancestors to modern events celebrating art and innovation, this page is your gateway to experiencing the heartbeat of Ghanaian culture.

Step into Ghana’s vibrant festivals, where traditions come alive with color, music, and unity—celebrating the heartbeat of our culture.

Festivals

Edina Bakatue Festival

Bakatue” translated means “the opening of the lagoon” or the “Draining of the Lagoon”. It is celebrated to commemorate the founding of the town, Elmina by the Europeans. It is also celebrated to invoke the deity, Nana Benya’s continuous protection of the state and its people. Read More

Fetu Afahye

This festival starts on the 1st of September every year, features of this festival is the state purification rites which includes the paramount Chief’s Yam festival and is observed in the form of offering mashed yams to the gods. Read More

Abeadze Ahobaa Kese festival

This annual festival is always celebrated, which commenced on the twelve (12th) Tuesday after ‘Abeadzeman Ogya’ is launched. It is a week-long celebration marked by traditional drumming and dancing with a set day for the performance of traditional rituals and special stool rites.Read More

Homowo Festival

The word Homowo (Homo – hunger, wo – hoot) can mean “to hoot (or jeer) at hunger” in the Ga language. The tradition of Homowo started with a period of hunger leading to famine due to failure of the seasonal rains needed by crops in the Greater Accra Region, where the Ga people predominantly dwell. When the rains returned to normal, the Ga people celebrated by creating the Homowo festival, hence its name and meaning.

Aboakyer Festival

Winneba Aboakyer is an ancient rite of animal sacrifice to the god Otu to celebrate their safe migration from the ancient western Sudan Empire to their present home, Winneba and also to remove evil and predict a good harvest. Their priest instructed them on behalf or their god, Otu to offer a young member of the Royal Family every year as a sacrifice.

Ohum Festival

Ohum festival is one of the famous and most important cultural festivals in the country and is celebrated annually by the people of the Okyeman Kingdom to thank God for his blessings and to cleanse the stools of Akyem.
